What Is Death Diaries?

We're not here for speedruns, flawless combos, or platinum trophies. We're here for the forgotten saves, the last-minute mistakes, the boss fights you almost won—and the tears (or laughter) that followed. Death Diaries is a curated blog that captures the most human moments in gaming—when control slips, when tension snaps, when the screen fades to black. These are not just failures; they are memories.

Founded by lifelong gamers who were tired of seeing "Game Over" treated as a dead end, this blog embraces the artistry of defeat. Each post is a eulogy, a confession, or a comedic breakdown of a moment that didn’t go as planned—but couldn’t have been scripted better. Some entries will make you laugh. Some might make you think. Others will hurt just enough to remind you why you loved that character, or why you’ll never trust that NPC again.

We celebrate the messy, emotional, and often hilarious reality of playing games—not to be perfect, but to be surprised, challenged, and ultimately moved. If you've ever stared at a loading screen in silence after losing someone or something in a game, you already understand what this is. Welcome to the place where your worst gaming moments become your best stories.

We Learn More When We Die

Victory is satisfying—but loss? Loss is instructive, emotional, and weirdly poetic. The best games make you care about what’s at stake. And when you lose, you feel it.
Whether it's the silence after your final breath in Inside, the slow descent in Return of the Obra Dinn, or the tactical tragedy in XCOM—you remember those moments.

We write to preserve them.
